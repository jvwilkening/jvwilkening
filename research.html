Research Interests

I am broadly interested in the physical and biological interactions that drive environmental processes in the Earth's critical zone. This has lead me to pursue a variety of research questions that sit at the intersection of hydrology, ecology, and biogeochemistry. See below for more information on some of these more recent questions I've been working on.

Modeling Plant Water Uptake

I am currently working on developing a coupled mdoel for plant water uptake that combines a model of plant hydraulics with a stable isotope tracking model. I am also conducting complementary greenhouse and field studies. Stay tuned for more updates as this work progresses!

Hydrologic Mechanisms of Plant Pathogen Spread

I developed a model to predict the biological growth and transport of the plant pathogen Phytophthora cinnamomi at the landscape scale. Using this model and case studies of disease progression in Western Australia and Spain, I investigated the role of overland runoff as a spread mechanism and how simple hydrological analysis can be used to create more quantitative and spatially-explicit management strategies for disease containment. A manuscript on this work is currently in preparation and will be submitted for publication soon.

Biogeochemical Cycling in Salt Marshes

During my time at the University of Cambridge, I studied interactions of sulfur, iron, and carbon cycling in salt marshes. As part of this work, I looked at seasonal dynamics of biogeochemical cycling and this work was published in coordination with other work from the Turchyn lab in early 2019. I also studied differences in the formation of volatile organosulfur compound formation between sulfidic and ferruginous sedimentary environments. This work is currently under revision.
